◎ Case Study · UX Design

Budget Mode
by Sheraz

Domain Fintech / Food-Tech UX
Platform Zomato / Swiggy
Type Feature Concept
Budget Mode ON
₹2,000 spent ₹1,000 left
⚠ 75% budget used — consider cheaper options?
01

Challenge

The Problem

92% of students and young professionals overspend on food delivery, driven by UI patterns that encourage impulse purchases — zero friction to add items, dark patterns hiding total cost, and no spending guardrails.

92% students face food-spend stress
02

Strategy

Research & Insight

Surveyed 60+ students and mapped spending patterns. Discovered a core insight: users don't lack awareness — they lack in-context friction. The solution isn't a separate budgeting app — it must live inside the order flow.

Insight: friction at point-of-decision
03

Execution

The Solution

Designed "Budget Mode" — a toggleable spend guardrail embedded into the cart and search flow. Smart alerts at 75% budget, budget-friendly item surfacing, and a real-time spending tracker. Figma prototypes with 3 user-test iterations.

3 Figma prototype iterations
04

Result

Outcome

User testing showed an 84% reduction in overspend intent when Budget Mode was active. Participants reported feeling "in control" without sacrificing convenience. Feature pitched as a retention mechanism for Zomato Pro subscribers.

84% reduction in overspend intent
